01

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains what information Sarvatra AI ("Sarvatra", "we", "the project") collects when you use the app, why it's collected, and how it's stored.

Information we collect

  • Account information: when you sign in with Google or create an email/password account, we receive your name, email address, and profile photo (if any) from the authentication provider.
  • Conversation data: the messages you send, the AI's responses, chat titles, and any files or images you attach are stored so your chat history is available across sessions.
  • App preferences: settings like your selected model, theme (light/dark), and response-speed preference.
  • Technical data: basic technical information (such as browser type) may be logged automatically for debugging and abuse prevention.

How we store it

Account authentication is handled by Firebase Authentication. Chats, messages, and settings are stored in Supabase, a hosted database provider, and are tied to your account so only you can access them. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties.

How AI messages are processed

When you send a message, it is forwarded to Groq's API (the provider that runs the Llama and other models available in Sarvatra) to generate a response. Groq processes this data to return the AI's reply; please refer to Groq's own privacy practices for how they handle inference requests. Sarvatra does not use your conversations to train its own models.

Your choices

  • You can delete individual chats or your entire chat history from within the app at any time.
  • You can request full account and data deletion by contacting us — we will remove your stored data within a reasonable time.
  • You can sign out at any time, which ends your active session.

Children's privacy

Sarvatra is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect data from them. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we'll remove it.

Changes to this policy

Because this is an actively developed student project, this policy may be updated as features change. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the latest version.

Questions about your data? Reach out via the contact details on the project's GitHub page or in-app support.

Cookie Policy

Sarvatra keeps things lightweight and does not use third-party advertising or tracking cookies.

What we actually use

  • Local storage: your theme preference (light/dark) is saved in your browser's local storage so it persists between visits.
  • Authentication session: Firebase Authentication and Supabase may set small session tokens (cookies or equivalent browser storage) purely to keep you signed in securely between page loads.
  • No advertising or analytics-tracking cookies are set by Sarvatra itself.

Third-party services

Fonts and icon libraries are loaded from CDNs (such as Google Fonts and cdnjs), which may set minimal technical cookies as part of standard content delivery. These are outside Sarvatra's direct control but are limited to functional delivery of assets, not tracking.

Managing storage

You can clear cookies and local storage at any time through your browser settings. Doing so will sign you out and reset your local theme preference, but will not delete your account data stored on the server.

AI Usage Policy / Responsible AI Policy

Sarvatra gives you access to third-party large language models (including Llama 4 and Llama 3.3 family models, served through Groq's infrastructure). This section explains how that access is intended to be used responsibly.

What Sarvatra does

  • Sarvatra itself does not train or fine-tune the models it offers — it's an interface that routes your prompts to Groq-hosted models and displays the responses.
  • No model access is exclusive to Sarvatra; the same underlying models are available through other providers.

Responsible use expectations

  • Treat AI responses as assistance, not as verified fact — always sanity-check anything used for decisions that matter (medical, legal, financial, academic submissions, etc.).
  • Don't use Sarvatra to generate content intended to deceive people into thinking it's human-created in contexts where that matters (e.g. passing off AI writing as your own academic work, where your institution prohibits it).
  • Don't attempt to use the AI to generate the categories of harmful content listed in the Content Policy below — Sarvatra relies on the underlying model providers' safety systems and will act on abuse reports.

Limitations

Language models can produce inaccurate, outdated, or biased output ("hallucinations"). Sarvatra does not guarantee the accuracy, completeness, or safety of any AI-generated response, and users are responsible for how they use that output.
